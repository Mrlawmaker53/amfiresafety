Some common questions
were often asked

What are some common causes of fires in homes?

The most common causes of fires in homes include cooking accidents, electrical malfunctions, heating equipment, and smoking.

How often should I test my smoke detectors?

It is recommended that you test your smoke detectors at least once a month to ensure they are functioning properly.

What should I do if there is a fire in my home?

If there is a fire in your home, the first priority is to get out safely and call 911. Do not try to put out a large fire by yourself.

How can I prevent fires in my home?

Some ways to prevent fires in your home include not leaving cooking unattended, keeping flammable materials away from heat sources, and ensuring your electrical systems are in good condition.

What should I do if my clothes catch fire?

If your clothes catch fire, stop, drop, and roll until the flames are out. Do not run as this can cause the fire to spread.

How can I prepare my family for a potential fire emergency?

You can prepare your family for a potential fire emergency by creating a fire escape plan and practicing it regularly, teaching children about fire safety, and ensuring you have working smoke detectors and fire extinguishers in your home.
